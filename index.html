Foundation Program

Swarpanthi Shadaj

6 Months Course (Sunday Batches)

Designed specifically for musical aspirants starting their journey. This course builds a strong foundation in notes, tempo, and scale recognition.

Course Curriculum Highlights:

  • Alankaar Foundation: Exercises 1 to 20 in slow tempo (with and without Harmonium using Tanpura)
  • Rhythm & Tempo: Alankaar 21 to 30; Mastery of Theka and Dugun beat counting
  • Raag Knowledge: Initial study of Raag Yaman, Raag Kafi, and Raag Bhairav
  • Taal Studies: Practical study of Teen Taal, Dadra, and Kahrawa
  • Ear Training: Notes (Swar) identification and Taali-Khali rhythm counting
  • Vocal Versatility: Basics of Bhajans, Kirtans, Folk and Ghazals

Intermediate Program

Swarpanthi Madhyam

Sunday Batches

For vocalists who have mastered basic pitches and wish to delve deeper into raag structuring, complex time cycles (taals), and harmonium self-accompaniment.

Course Curriculum Highlights:

  • Intermediate Alankaar: Exercises 1 to 20 in medium tempo with Tanpura
  • Expanded Raag Knowledge: Study of Raag Yaman, Kafi, Bhairav, Khamaj, Bihag, Bhoopali, and Bilawal
  • Improvisation: Detailed practical learning of Avaroh, Pakad, Swarmalika, and Chhota Khayal
  • Taal Expansion: Ektaal, Jhaptaal, Rupak, Deepchandi in addition to basic taals
  • Scale Formation: Key transposition and root note recognition
  • Notation Writing: Writing and reading notations accurately using classical symbols

Advanced Program

Swarpanthi Pancham

Sunday Batches

Our highest tier designed for experts. Focuses on advanced vocal aesthetics, raga elaboration, Vilambit khayals, complex bol-taans, and stage performance preparation.

Course Curriculum Highlights:

  • Vilambit Khayal: Slow-tempo compositions with detailed improvisation
  • Vocal Techniques: Fast-paced Taans, complex Alaaps, and Boltaans
  • Complex Taals: Mastering Ada Chautaal, Jhumra, Dhamar, and more
  • Performance Aesthetics: Stage presence, expression control, and ragas mood mapping
  • Riyaz Mastery: Professional practice strategies for vocal preservation and stamina
  • Self-Accompaniment: Advanced Harmonium playing alongside vocal performance

Getting Ready

Online Classroom Requirements

To ensure the highest quality of audio and clear feedback during vocal training sessions, we recommend students prepare the following setup:

  • Stable Internet Connection

    A minimum of 5 Mbps upload/download speed for seamless video and low-latency audio transmission.

  • Webcam-enabled Device

    A laptop, tablet, or desktop computer. Visual feedback is crucial for correcting sitting posture and breathing methods.

  • Headphones or Earphones

    Essential to prevent echo and feedback loops during live sessions, ensuring you hear the Tanpura clearly.

  • Electronic Tanpura App

    Apps like iShala, Tanpura Droid, or a physical electronic Tanpura configured to your vocal scale.

  • Quiet & Distraction-free Space

    A peaceful environment allows you to sing freely without external noises interfering with your microphone.
